Games

A look at some of the games that inspire & challenge me

Dice Throne

Dice throne is a strategic card and dice game. Players choose from 16 unique heroes, each with distinctive aesthetics and abilities, and compete to be the last one standing.

Often described to new players as "Yahtzee meets Magic: The Gathering", Dice Throne is a fast and strategic game of card play and dice manipulation for 2-6 Players. Its variety, set up time, playtime, and addictive nature make it one of the easiest games in existence to get to the table. Dice Throne will be the workhorse of your game collection. Dice Throne can be played as a 1vs1 dueling game, in teams, and even cooperatively with the upcoming Dice Throne Adventures. Dice Throne features 16 heroes to choose from, each featuring wildly different aesthetics and play styles.

I love playing Dice Throne because it has a good balance of luck & skill, and the variety of heroes makes every match much more exciting. I often play the co-op version more than pvp, as there is a lot more to it. It's a great time to relax and play with friends!

Satisfactory

Satisfactory is a first-person open-world factory building video game with a dash of exploration and combat, where you design and automate production lines on an alien planet. You can play alone or with friends, explore the alien planet, create mutli-story factories, and enter conveyor belt heaven!

The reason why I chose this game is because it involves a lot of thinking and planning, which I believe is critical for me, as I am a computer science student. There is a lot of creativity and engineering challenges it provides that makes it feel worth it once you work out the problem(s).
