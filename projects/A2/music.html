My Music Journey

It's 2016. Right as I was graduating high school, my friend (Chudi) and I were hanging out in his basement. I just arrived at his house and noticed an unfamiliar software he had showing on his computer. "FL Studio", he said. I think he knew that I was confused as I had a puzzled look to me. I asked him what that software was for and he said it was "for making beats."

He showed me how it worked and some of the beats he was working on. It looked so simple, yet so... complex. Of course, it was beginner beats so it was nothing too crazy. The mix was poor, and the sounds seemed to clash against each other, but that's the beauty of making music. I decided to pick this hobby up of making music, and it changed my life forever.

I started off learning FL Studio by watching a ton of YouTube tutorials. There were plenty of guides that helped me become more aware of what I was doing and having a purpose behind it. Some of my first few tracks were not very good, as expected. Mixes were poor, meaning sounds were louder than others and sometimes the lower end Hz had too much. This is where I learned how to use EQ (Equalization). This is essentially taking slices of the frequency spectrum and amplyifing (or diminishing) them until the overall sound is more pleasing. This changed everything I knew about making music. I could finally make something that sounds decent.

Throughout the years since 2016, I've made countless tracks that have never been uploaded, and will never be. I call these "ghost tracks". I believe making these are more important than watching tutorials forever. Sure, they'll never be heard by anyone but you, but it's the learning experience of what works and what doesn't that helped make me become a better producer.

Fast forward to today, I know so much more than I did a year ago. I still love making music, and I am trying to shift into making tracks for other people. I love helping other musicians achieve their goals of creating music, as I once was by people online.
