Learn the System

Understand the turn before chasing big combos.

Dice Throne feels much easier once the turn order clicks. This page turns the rules into a cleaner sequence so a new player can understand setup, pacing, and where important decisions actually happen.

Quick setup

  • Choose a hero and place their board, tokens, dice, and deck within reach.
  • Set starting Health and Combat Points using the hero leaflet.
  • Draw the starting hand, shuffle the deck, and roll to determine the first player.
  • Keep your status effects visible from the start so each turn is easier to track.

Complete turn order

  1. Upkeep Phase: resolve start-of-turn status effects, lingering triggers, and passive hero abilities.
  2. Income Phase: gain 1 CP and draw 1 card so you can plan the turn with fresh resources.
  3. Main Phase One: play upgrades, action cards, or sell cards for more CP before rolling.
  4. Offensive Roll Phase: roll up to three times and lock in the offensive ability you want to activate.
  5. Targeting and Resolve: declare the target, apply modifiers, and resolve the attack sequence.
  6. Defensive Roll Phase: the defender rolls if the attack is defendable and applies shields, prevention, or return effects.
  7. Main Phase Two: use a final window to play cards, upgrades, or cleanup actions after combat resolves.
  8. Discard Phase: sell down to hand limit if needed and carry remaining setup into the next round.

New-player shortcut

If you remember only one thing, remember this: the turn is not just about rolling well. Good turns are built in the main phases before and after the attack.
That mindset makes the rest of the rules much easier to learn.
Learn one hero first

Repeated reps with a single hero teach timing, reroll priorities, and how to spend CP more effectively than constantly switching characters.

Think in phases

Most mistakes happen when players forget card windows. Main Phase One and Main Phase Two are where a lot of subtle value lives.

Announce the attack

Saying “ready to attack” after your final roll keeps the table aligned and makes defensive interactions much smoother.
