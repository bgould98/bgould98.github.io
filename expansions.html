Beyond Duels

Expand the game into campaigns, villains, and co-op missions.

One of the best things about Dice Throne is how far the system stretches. The expansions take the core dice-and-cards engine and turn it into bigger experiences without losing what makes the base game fun.

Campaign Style

Dice Throne Adventures

Dice Throne Adventures transforms the base game into a cooperative dungeon crawl for 1 to 4 players. Instead of only dueling, your party explores maps, fights waves of enemies, collects treasure, and prepares for boss encounters.

The expansion keeps your favorite hero boards and dice at the center of the experience, but reframes them around teamwork, longer arcs, and progression that feels more like a campaign.

Adventures overview

Marvel Co-op

Marvel Dice Throne Missions

Missions brings solo and cooperative scenario play to the Marvel roster. Heroes team up against villain-led encounters, complete objectives, and gain perks that help carry momentum into tougher fights.

It keeps the familiar hero systems in place, but adds a more directed objective structure so the match feels less like pure player-versus-player combat and more like a comic-book boss run.

Missions how-to guide

Which expansion is right for you?

  • Choose Adventures if you want dungeon-crawl structure, loot, and a broader campaign feeling.
  • Choose Missions if you want fast Marvel-themed co-op scenarios with villain pressure and perk progression.
  • Stick with the base game if your group mainly wants clean, replayable duels with lots of hero variety.

Why expansions matter

The expansions work because they do not replace the hero system. They recontextualize it, letting the same characters feel fresh in a completely different kind of challenge.
That is what makes Dice Throne's ecosystem feel broader than a standard duel game.
